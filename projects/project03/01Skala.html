Mathias Eick - Skala

2020.1

Listening to Mathias Eick's Skala, I was sent back to that sweep of water.

The sky is of violet colour, and the sea is in dark-blue.

At sunset, a honk echoes faintly in the distance, with a cruise ship dawdling slowly into the harbor. The sky darkens, and the misty mountains in the distance blend into the sea. The corporal figure of the rocks at the seaside hid itself into the shadow of the seas, and melts its way into the mist.

I sat on the promenade of the dock and saw the wave shifting in front of me. The wave was shifting, the boats in the dock were shifting. Mountains and the cruise ships afar seem to also undulate with them. The rhythm was regular and persistent, that for a moment, I cannot tell whether what moves was the sea or the ground beneath me.

The moon rises. Sky once again found itself a pale violet. In the dock, lamps gradually fill up with light; and the sound of toasting rises and falls across the yachts. The lights twinkle upon water, imitating the reflections of stars.

Having grown up by the sea, Mathias Eick and his friends brings us the visuals, tactiles and sounds of the Norwegian sea - the vessels, the sky, the fjords, and the generosity and cosmopolitanism of people by the sea, and of course, the infinite shifting of the water. Aside of Biermann, track Oslo is also an portray of life of the seaside city.

Here, the usually shrill trumpet sounds like a foghorn, as if standing by the sea and hearing a sound from within the depths of a fjord.

听着Mathias Eick 的Skala，我仿佛又回到了那一泓海水之上。

天是粉紫色的，海是深蓝色的。

太阳下山了，一艘游轮在远处微弱地鸣笛，缓缓驶进海港。天色暗了，远处的群山变得雾蒙蒙的，和海水融为一处。岩石硕大的身躯藏在海的暗影里，在雾气里化开了。

我坐在港口船坞的步道上，眼前是晃动的海水。海水在晃动，船坞里的船在晃动；眼前的群山和轮船似也跟着波动起来。那摇晃是如此地规律和持久，以至于一时间我分不清晃动的到底是海水还是脚下的地面。

月亮升上来了。天空又重新找回黯淡的粉紫色。船坞里，灯火逐渐点起，响起船上人们交杯碰盏的声音。灯光映射到海水上，海面上闪烁着晃动的星光。

在海边长大的人，Mathias Eick 和他的小伙伴们，给我们带来了挪威海边的视觉，触觉和听觉。船，天空，巨大的峡湾，海边人的宽广豁达，都收入其中，当然其中最重要的还是那一泓无限晃动的海水。除Biermann之外，Oslo一曲更是海边都会人生活面貌的具现。

在这里，刺耳的小号被吹得像雾角一样，像是站在海边，听到远处峡湾深处传来的声音。
